Objective of CBB Bluechips

The winner will be crowned shortly after the championship game has been completed and will be awarded to the person with the highest net worth. Each person who plays, is tasked with the challenge of turning $100,000 of fake cash, into the highest amount they can. During the tournament, a total of 67games will be played over a few week span. Each of those games is an opportunity to increase your net worth.

Point-spread


EVERY 1 POINT THAT BEATS THE SPREAD WILL
RAISE THE TEAM'S VALUE BY $200 IN GAME PLAY CASH

EVERY 1 POINT THAT LOOSES THE SPREAD WILL
LOWER THE TEAM'S VALUE BY $200 IN GAME PLAY CASH


The point spread is sometimes known as an equalizer for sportsbook operators. All teams arenâ€™t created equally, so sportsbooks can create a point spread for a game so that each team playing has an almost even chance of winning the game. In a way, the point spread will even the field for both teams. With that being said, having a stake in a team that has recently beat the spread, will soon increase your net worth. On the flip side, owning a team that failed to "Cover", will take a hit to your net worth.
NOTE: The team with the negative point spread is always the favorite to win.

On the market page, when browsing for a team to put some ownership into, you may notice something that looks like: "(-4.5) VS: XXXXXXX". What this tells you is the point spread for next upcoming game they are involved in. Lets say you are looking at buying Duke, and it says that. That means, in order for Dukes value to rise, they must win by more than 4.5 points. So hypothetically, if they win 65-55, they cover. If they only win 65-63, they fail to cover. If they lose, the failed to cover.

Market

Once you are ready to buy some shares, the place to go is the market.

The photo above is an example of one of the many teams available for purchasing. Here you can see the team name, Current market price per 1 share, who they play if the have a game that day, an interactive chart on historical pricing by round, a trade button, a seed identifier(1-16), and the shares outstanding.

New this year is the feature that displays the point spread and who the next opponent is. If you hover the mouse over the bar, you will either see Favorite or Underdog. In the example above, Oklahoma State plays West Virgina next and is favored by 5 points. It is red as a caution that if you purchase any shares of this team, they must not only win, they must win by 5 whole points. If the number is positive, it will be green, and it stands for a losing buffer.

Shares Outstanding is a way for the user to know just how many share are owned throughout the whole CBB Community. With the picture above, it says "Shares Outstanding: 19". That means every user collectively owns a totals of 19 shares.

The interactive chart shows the price history of the team. In this example, they have yet to play a game and only have the starting price registered.

One quick thing to note, you may have noticed the img is surrounded by a green border. The green border signals that you as the user have a stake in that team. Once you sell your share(s), the green border will not appear.

Trading

There are multiple ways to end up on the trading screen. The first is to click the blue trade button on a team while visiting the market. Another way is to click the trade button while looking at your portfolio. If you are on a small device such as a phone, you can click the Team's Logo on the portfolio page to open up the trade screen.
