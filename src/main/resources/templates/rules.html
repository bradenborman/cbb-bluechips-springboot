Objective of CBB Bluechips

The winner will be crowned shortly after the championship game has been completed and will be awarded to the person with the highest net worth. Each person who plays, is tasked with the challenge of turning $100,000 of fake cash, into the highest amount they can. During the tournament, a total of 67games will be played over a few week span. Each of those games is an opportunity to increase your net worth.

Understanding the Point spread

The point spread is sometimes known as an equalizer for sportsbook operators. All teams arenâ€™t created equally, so sportsbooks can create a point spread for a game so that each team playing has an almost even chance of winning the game. In a way, the point spread will even the field for both teams. With that being said, having a stake in a team that has recently beat the spread, will soon increase your net worth. On the flip side, owning a team that failed to "Cover", will take a hit to your net worth.
NOTE: The team with the negative point spread is always the favorite to win.

On the market page, when browsing for a team to put some ownership into, you may notice something that looks like: "(-4.5) VS: XXXXXXX". What this tells you is the point spread for next upcoming game they are involved in. Lets say you are looking at buying Duke, and it says that. That means, in order for Dukes value to rise, they must win by more than 4.5 points. So hypothetically, if they win 65-55, they cover. If they only win 65-63, they fail to cover. If they lose, the failed to cover.
